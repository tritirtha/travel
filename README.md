---

## Travel Philosophy

These itineraries are designed around a **hub-and-spoke model**:

- **Long drives** to reach destinations (600-800 km on travel days)
- **Stay 2-3 nights** at each campsite
- **Park the campervan** and explore by car
- **Day trips** from campsite bases (50-150 km round trips)
- **Maximum 200-400 km** between campsite moves

This approach provides:
‚úì Relaxed exploration without daily packing  
‚úì Lower fuel consumption during exploration days  
‚úì Time to enjoy each region  
‚úì Flexibility for weather and spontaneity  
‚úì Better campsite experience (worth settling in)

## Essential Information

### Your Vehicle: Peugeot 5008 1.6 THP (2015)

**Towing Capacity:** Check your specific model  
- Typically: 1,300-1,650 kg
- Ensure campervan weight is within limits

**Towing Tips:**
- Add 30-50% to journey times
- Fuel consumption increases 40-60%
- Mountain passes will be slow
- Practice reversing with trailer before trip
- Check tire pressure regularly
- Use low gears on descents

### Your Inflatable Kayak

**Perfect For:**
- Calm lakes (all itineraries)
- Protected bays and fjords
- River sections

**Be Careful With:**
- Open ocean/sea (Atlantic, North Scotland)
- High winds
- Strong currents
- Cold water (wetsuit recommended)

**Always:**
- Wear life jackets
- Check weather conditions
- Let someone know your plans
- Bring dry bag for camera gear

### Drone Photography

**Registration Required:**
- EU countries: Register in one EU country (valid for all)
- UK: Separate registration (CAA)
- Norway: Separate registration

**Best Locations:**
- Norwegian fjords (permissive, dramatic)
- Scottish Highlands (remote areas)
- Adriatic coastline (check local rules)
- Alpine lakes (restricted in some areas)

**Avoid:**
- Airports and military zones
- National parks (often restricted)
- Crowded tourist areas
- High winds (especially mountains/coast)

### Camping Strategy

**Wild Camping Legal:**
- Norway, Sweden, Scotland (with limitations)
- Generally NOT allowed: Germany, Switzerland, Croatia, France

**Campsite Booking:**
- Peak season (July-August): Book ahead
- Shoulder season: Usually available without booking
- Use apps: Park4Night, Campercard, iOverlander

**Facilities:**
- Most campsites: Showers, electricity, kitchen, WiFi
- Costs: ‚Ç¨20-60/night depending on country
- Wild camping: Free but fewer facilities

### Documents Needed

- Passports
- International Driving Permit (non-EU countries)
- Vehicle registration
- Insurance (including trailer coverage)
- European Health Insurance Card (EHIC)
- Breakdown cover (ANWB or similar)

**In Vehicle:**
- Warning triangles
- Reflective vests
- First aid kit
- Headlamp beam deflectors (UK)
- Country stickers

**Vignettes Required:**
- Switzerland: CHF 40 (annual)
- Austria: ‚Ç¨9.90 (10-day)
- Slovenia: ‚Ç¨15 (weekly)

## Seasonal Recommendations

### Spring (March-May)
**Best:** 
- Adriatic Coast (May)
- French/Spanish Atlantic (May-June)

**Pros:** Fewer crowds, flowers blooming, moderate prices  
**Cons:** Mountain passes may be closed, cooler water

### Summer (June-August)
**Best:**
- Norwegian Fjords (midnight sun!)
- Scottish Highlands
- Alpine Lakes

**Pros:** Best weather, all roads open, warmest water  
**Cons:** Crowded, expensive, need to book ahead

### Fall (September-October)
**Best:**
- Adriatic Coast
- French/Spanish Atlantic
- Alpine Lakes (September)

**Pros:** Beautiful colors, fewer crowds, good deals  
**Cons:** Shorter days, some mountain passes closing, cooling water

### Winter (November-February)
**Not recommended** for campervan travel to these destinations  
- Many mountain passes closed
- Cold camping conditions
- Limited facilities open
- Challenging driving conditions

## Money-Saving Tips

1. **Stock up before expensive countries:**
   - Buy groceries in Germany before Norway/Switzerland
   - Fuel up in Denmark before Norway
   - Stock wine in France before UK

2. **Cook at campsites:**
   - Camping kitchens available
   - Save ‚Ç¨30-50/day vs. restaurants
   - Still eat out for local specialties

3. **Free activities:**
   - Hiking is free everywhere
   - Many viewpoints free
   - Beach access mostly free
   - Wild camping where legal

4. **Shoulder season travel:**
   - May, June, September, October
   - 30-50% cheaper than peak summer
   - Better weather than you think!

5. **Overnight ferries:**
   - Save one night's accommodation
   - Rest while traveling
   - Worth the extra cost

6. **Apps for deals:**
   - Park4Night (free/cheap camping)
   - GasBuddy (fuel prices)
   - Too Good To Go (discounted food)

## Safety Considerations

### Driving
- Adjust to local driving styles
- Single-track roads: Use passing places
- Mountains: Take it slow, low gears
- Fatigue: Take breaks every 2 hours

### Kayaking
- Always check weather forecast
- Tell someone your plans
- Don't overestimate abilities
- Cold water = hypothermia risk
- Life jackets non-negotiable

### Hiking
- Check trail conditions
- Weather can change rapidly (mountains)
- Tell someone your route
- Turn back if conditions deteriorate
- Carry extra food/water

### Wildlife
- **Scotland:** Watch for deer on roads (dusk/dawn)
- **Norway:** Rare but moose exist
- **Bears:** Not in these regions
- **Respect all wildlife:** Don't approach, don't feed

## Final Tips

1. **Be flexible:** Weather will dictate some activities
2. **Don't overplan:** Leave room for spontaneity and rest
3. **Quality over quantity:** Better to explore fewer places deeply
4. **Respect campsite quiet hours:** Usually 22:00-07:00
5. **Learn basic phrases:** "Hello," "thank you," "where is..."
6. **Document everything:** But also put the camera down sometimes
7. **Backup photos daily:** Don't lose precious memories
8. **Meet locals:** They know the best spots
9. **Try local food:** Part of the experience
10. **Enjoy the journey:** It's not just about destinations
