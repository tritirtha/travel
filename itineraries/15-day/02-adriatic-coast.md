# Adriatic Coast Explorer - 15 Days

**Best Season:** May-June or September-October (Avoid peak summer crowds/heat)  
**Total Campervan Distance:** ~5,200 km  
**Driving Style:** Long initial/return drives (600-700 km), hub stays (2-3 nights)  
**Campsites:** Strategic coastal bases with car day trips

---

## Overview

Experience the stunning Adriatic coastline through Slovenia, Croatia, and optional Bosnia. Crystal-clear turquoise waters, dramatic limestone cliffs, ancient walled cities, and spectacular national parks. Perfect for sea kayaking, island exploration, and coastal photography.

## CAMPSITE HUB #1: Lake Bled Area, Slovenia
**Location:** Bled  
**Nights:** 2 nights (Days 2-3)  
**Campervan Stays Put**

### Day 3: Lake Bled & Vintgar Gorge (Local Exploration)
**Car Round Trip:** ~50 km

#### Morning: Kayaking Lake Bled
- **Rental:** Multiple locations around lake
- **Your inflatable:** Perfect for this lake!
- **Paddle to island:** 
  - Church with 99 steps
  - Ring the wishing bell
- **Alpine setting:** Mountains surrounding
- **Duration:** 2-3 hours
- **Conditions:** Very calm, perfect for beginners

#### Late Morning: Bled Castle
- **Perched:** On 130m cliff above lake
- **Entry:** €15/person
- **Views:** Best lake panorama
- **Museum:** Printing press demonstration
- **1 hour visit**

#### Afternoon: Vintgar Gorge
- **Location:** 4 km from Bled
- **Entry:** €10/person (peak season)
- **Walk:** 1.6 km boardwalk through canyon
- **Crystal-clear river:** Radovna
- **Waterfalls and pools**
- **Photography:** Stunning natural formations
- **Duration:** 2 hours

#### Optional
- **Bled Toboggan:** Summer toboggan run
- **Lake swimming:** Designated areas
- **Viewpoint hike:** Ojstrica or Mala Osojnica (30-45 min hike for THE Bled photo)

## CAMPSITE HUB #2: Rovinj, Croatia (Istrian Peninsula)
**Location:** Rovinj  
**Nights:** 3 nights (Days 4-6)  
**Move:** 250 km from Lake Bled

### Day 4: Slovenia → Croatia + Arrival
**Campervan Drive:** 250 km (3-4 hours)

#### Border Crossing
- **Slovenia → Croatia:** EU member (easy crossing)
- **Currency change:** Croatia uses Euro (as of 2023)
- **Vignette:** Not needed in Croatia (toll booths instead)

#### Optional En Route Stop
- **Postojna Cave:** Slovenia
  - 24 km underground chambers
  - Train ride into cave
  - €30/person
  - 2-3 hours total
  - Worthwhile detour!

#### Afternoon: Rovinj Arrival
- Arrive Croatian coast
- Setup campsite
- **Evening walk:** Rovinj old town
  - Venetian architecture
  - Colorful harbor
  - St. Euphemia Church
  - Narrow cobblestone streets

#### Campsite Options
- **Polari Campsite:** Large, near Rovinj, good facilities
- **Amarin Campsite:** Alternative, direct beach access
- €35-45/night

### Day 5: Rovinj & Coastal Exploration (Day Trip)
**Car Round Trip:** ~80 km

#### Morning: Sea Kayaking Rovinj
- **Launch:** Rovinj harbor or beaches
- **Explore:**
  - Coastal caves and coves
  - Red Island (Sveti Andrija)
  - Golden Cape (Zlatni Rt) forest park from sea
- **Conditions:** Generally calm, Adriatic perfect for kayaking
- **Duration:** 3-4 hours
- **Guided tours available:** Or explore independently

#### Afternoon: Istrian Coast
**Option A: Pula**
- 40 km south
- **Roman Amphitheater:** 
  - One of best-preserved in world
  - Still hosts concerts/events
  - €10 entry
- **Temple of Augustus**
- **Brijuni Islands:** Optional boat trip

**Option B: Poreč**
- 30 km north
- **UNESCO Euphrasian Basilica:**
  - 6th-century mosaics
  - €8 entry
- **Old town:** Roman ruins, Venetian houses

#### Evening
- Return to Rovinj
- Sunset from town walls
- Seafood dinner (Croatian cuisine excellent!)

### Day 6: Istrian Inland (Day Trip by Car)
**Car Round Trip:** ~150 km

#### Inland Istria - Hilltop Towns
- **Motovun:**
  - Medieval walled town on hilltop
  - Truffle capital of Croatia
  - Walk ramparts (360° views)
  - 1-2 hours
  
- **Grožnjan:**
  - Artist village
  - Galleries and workshops
  - Tiny, charming
  - 30 minutes

- **Hum:**
  - World's smallest town (30 residents!)
  - Photo opportunity
  - 15 minutes

#### Wine Tasting (Optional)
- Multiple wineries in region
- Malvazija (white wine specialty)

#### Photography
- Rolling hills
- Vineyard landscapes  
- Medieval hilltop towns
- Very different from coastal scenes

## CAMPSITE HUB #3: Zadar / Šibenik Area
**Location:** Šibenik or between Zadar/Šibenik  
**Nights:** 3 nights (Days 7-9)  
**Move:** 300 km from Rovinj

### Day 7: Istria → Dalmatia + Plitvice Lakes
**Campervan Drive:** 300 km (4-5 hours + park visit)

#### Morning: Drive Inland to Plitvice
- Leave Rovinj by 8:00 AM
- Drive 150 km to Plitvice Lakes National Park

#### Plitvice Lakes National Park (UNESCO)
- **Entry:** €20-40/person (varies by season)
- **Time:** 4-6 hours (various routes)
- **16 terraced lakes** connected by waterfalls
- **Boardwalks:** Over and beside water
- **Swimming:** Not allowed (protected)
- **No kayaking:** Protected area
- **Photography:**
  - Cascading waterfalls
  - Emerald/turquoise pools
  - Walkways over water
  - Incredibly photogenic!
- **Drone:** Prohibited in park

#### Afternoon: Continue to Coast
- Drive 150 km to Šibenik area
- Arrive late afternoon/evening
- Setup campsite

#### Campsite
- **Solaris Campsite:** Near Šibenik, large, good facilities
- **Alternative:** Campsites between Šibenik and Primošten
- €30-40/night

### Day 8: Krka National Park (Day Trip)
**Car Round Trip:** ~40 km

#### Krka National Park
- **Entry:** €15-30/person (seasonal)
- **Main attraction:** Skradinski Buk waterfall
  - 800m wide cascade
  - Multiple levels
  - Boardwalk circuit (1-2 hours)
- **Swimming:** ALLOWED at certain spots! (unlike Plitvice)
  - Bring swimsuit
  - Cool but refreshing
- **Boat trip:** Optional extension to Visovac Island monastery

#### Kayaking
- **Krka River kayaking:** Possible from various points
- Calm water sections
- Scenic canyon areas

#### Afternoon: Šibenik Old Town
- **UNESCO Cathedral:** St. James Cathedral (unique stone construction)
- **St. Michael's Fortress:** Sunset views
- **Narrow streets:** Medieval layout
- **Less touristy** than Split/Dubrovnik

#### Photography
- Waterfalls (long exposure opportunities)
- Historical architecture
- Coastal town at dusk

### Day 9: Zadar & Islands (Day Trip by Car)
**Car Round Trip:** ~80 km + ferry option

#### Morning: Zadar
- 50 km north of Šibenik
- **Sea Organ:**
  - Musical steps play with waves
  - Unique sound sculpture
  - Free
- **Greeting to the Sun:**
  - Solar-powered light installation
  - 22m diameter circle
  - Beautiful at sunset
- **Old Town:** Roman forum, churches

#### Afternoon Options

**Option A: Kornati Islands (Full Day Excursion)**
- **Book ahead:** Boat tour from Zadar
- **Kornati National Park:** 89 islands
- **Full-day boat trip:** €50-80/person
- **Includes:** Swimming, snorkeling, lunch
- **Swimming/snorkeling** in crystal-clear water

**Option B: Nin**
- 15 km from Zadar
- **Smallest cathedral** in world
- **Sandy beaches** (rare in Croatia!)
- **Salt pans**
- **Kayaking:** Shallow lagoons, perfect for exploring

**Option C: Stay in Šibenik**
- **Relax at campsite**
- **Local beaches**
- **Catch up on photos/videos**

## CAMPSITE HUB #4: Split / Makarska Area
**Location:** Between Split and Makarska  
**Nights:** 3 nights (Days 10-12)  
**Move:** 150 km from Šibenik

### Day 10: Travel to Split + Exploration
**Campervan Drive:** 90 km to Split (1.5 hours)

#### Afternoon: Split Old Town
- Setup campsite (near Split or continue to Makarska)
- **Diocletian's Palace (UNESCO):**
  - 1,700-year-old Roman palace
  - Now the old town (people live inside!)
  - Cellars, temples, cathedral
  - Game of Thrones filming location
- **Riva (waterfront promenade):** 
  - Cafes and palm trees
- **Marjan Hill:** 
  - Forest park overlooking city
  - Sunset viewpoint
- **3-4 hours exploration**

#### Campsite Options
- **Near Split:** Multiple options
- **Or continue 60 km to Makarska area** (recommended)
  - More scenic
  - Better for next days' activities
- €30-40/night

### Day 11: Islands - Brač or Hvar (Full Day Trip)
**Ferry required + car driving on island**

#### Option A: Brač Island
- **Ferry:** Split to Supetar (50 min, €6-8/person + car)
- **Zlatni Rat Beach:**
  - Golden Horn
  - Most photographed beach in Croatia
  - Unique triangular shape extends into sea
  - Shape changes with currents!
  - Excellent swimming
- **Bol:** Charming town
- **Vidova Gora:** Highest point in Adriatic islands (778m)
  - Drive or hike up
  - Spectacular views

#### Option B: Hvar Island  
- **Ferry:** Split to Stari Grad (2 hours, €10-12/person + car)
- **Most luxurious Croatian island**
- **Hvar Town:**
  - Venetian architecture
  - Fortress with views
  - Luxury yachts in harbor
  - Lavender fields (peak: June-July)
- **Hvar is busier/pricier** than Brač

#### Kayaking
- **Both islands:** Excellent sea kayaking
- **Rental available** at beaches/towns
- **Explore coves and caves**

#### Photography/Drone
- Aerial shots of beaches
- Turquoise water from above
- Medieval towns from hilltops

### Day 12: Makarska Riviera (Day Trip by Car)
**Car Round Trip:** ~100 km

#### Morning: Makarska
- **Beach town** beneath Biokovo mountains
- **Dramatic backdrop:** Mountains meet sea
- **Pebble beaches:** Clear water

#### Kayaking Makarska Coast
- **Launch:** Makarska beaches
- **Paddle:** Along coast, explore coves
- **Conditions:** Generally calm, protected
- **Duration:** 2-4 hours

#### Afternoon: Biokovo Nature Park
- **Drive:** Panoramic road up mountains

## Practical Tips

### Driving in Croatia
- **Tolls:** Pay-as-you-go (credit cards accepted)
- **Roads:** Generally excellent condition
- **Coastal road:** Scenic but slower than highway
- **Parking:** Can be challenging in old towns
  - Use park-and-ride or campsite shuttle

### Kayaking Specifics
- **Adriatic Sea:** Very calm, high visibility
- **Water temperature:** 20-25°C in summer (comfortable!)
- **Salt water:** More buoyant than freshwater
- **Perfect for amateurs:** Protected bays and coves
- **Rental widely available:** €10-20/hour
- **Guided tours:** €40-80 for half-day

### Swimming
- **Beaches:** Mostly pebble/rocky (water shoes helpful)
- **Water clarity:** Exceptional visibility
- **Facilities:** Showers, changing rooms at paid beaches
- **Free beaches:** Everywhere along coast

### Photography & Drone
- **Drone regulations (Croatia):**
  - Drones <500g: No registration needed
  - Heavier: Register online (simple process)
  - Avoid airports, military zones
  - Restricted in national parks
- **Best shots:**
  - Aerial coastline and islands
  - Walled cities from above
  - Waterfalls (long exposure)
  - Sunset over Adriatic
- **Golden hour:** Amazing Mediterranean light

### Food & Drink
- **Cuisine:** Mediterranean/Italian influence
- **Seafood:** Fresh and excellent
- **Specialties:**
  - Peka (slow-cooked meat/vegetables)
  - Black risotto (squid ink)
  - Pašticada (Dalmatian beef stew)
- **Wine:** Excellent local varieties
- **Olive oil:** Some of world's best
