# Alpine Lakes Circuit - 10 Days

**Best Season:** June-September (Mountain passes open, warm weather)  
**Total Campervan Distance:** ~2,900 km  
**Driving Style:** Long initial/return drives (600-700 km), short moves between hubs (150-250 km)  
**Campsites:** 2-3 nights each, car day trips from base

---

## Overview

Experience the most stunning alpine lakes of Switzerland, Italy, and Austria using strategic campsite hubs. Park your campervan and explore turquoise glacial lakes, dramatic mountain scenery, and charming alpine villages by car. Perfect for kayaking in crystal-clear waters and capturing breathtaking mountain photography.

---

## CAMPSITE HUB #1: Lake Constance (Bodensee)
**Location:** Konstanz or Meersburg, Germany  
**Nights:** 2 nights (Days 2-3)  
**Campervan Stays Put**

### Day 2: Arrival & Lake Constance Introduction
**Campervan Drive:** 150 km from Black Forest  
**Arrive:** Late morning

#### Afternoon Activities
- Setup at campsite
- Walk around Konstanz old town (if on German side)
- Evening lakeside stroll
- **Photography:** Lake with Alps in background

#### Campsite Options
- **Campingplatz Klausenhorn:** Directly on lake, excellent facilities
- **Naturcamping Litzelstetten:** Quieter, beautiful setting

### Day 3: Lake Constance Exploration (Day Trip by Car)
**Car Round Trip:** ~120 km

#### Morning: Island Hopping
- **Mainau Island** (Flower Island)
  - 45 hectares of gardens
  - Palm house, butterfly house
  - Spring/summer: Millions of tulips/roses
  - **Photography:** Botanical paradise

#### Afternoon: Kayaking
- **Launch:** Multiple spots around lake
  - Konstanz harbor area
  - Meersburg (medieval town backdrop)
- **Conditions:** Very calm, perfect for beginners
- **Water:** Clear, greenish-blue
- **Duration:** 2-4 hours

#### Alternative Activities
- **Meersburg:** Medieval castle, wine terraces
- **Überlingen:** Historic spa town
- **Pfahlbauten Museum:** Prehistoric lake dwellings (UNESCO)

#### Photography/Drone
- Lake panoramas with Swiss/Austrian Alps
- Colorful lakeside towns
- Vineyards sloping to water
- **Drone note:** Check local restrictions near towns

## CAMPSITE HUB #2: Interlaken Region, Switzerland
**Location:** Interlaken or Lauterbrunnen  
**Nights:** 3 nights (Days 4-6)  
**Move:** 250 km from Lake Constance

### Day 4: Travel to Switzerland + Lauterbrunnen
**Campervan Drive:** 250 km (3-4 hours)

#### Border Crossing
- **IMPORTANT:** Buy Swiss vignette at border (CHF 40 for year)
- Mandatory for all motorways

#### Afternoon
- Arrive Interlaken/Lauterbrunnen area
- Setup campsite
- **Lauterbrunnen Valley:**
  - 72 waterfalls in one valley!
  - Staubbach Falls (300m) in village
  - Walk valley floor (easy, spectacular)

#### Evening
- Swiss dinner (fondue or raclette if eating out)
- Prepare for mountain adventures

#### Campsite Options
- **Camping Jungfrau:** In Lauterbrunnen, surrounded by waterfalls
- **Lazy Rancho:** Alternative, good facilities
- **Cost:** CHF 45-60/night (expensive but worth it)

### Day 5: Lake Brienz & Waterfalls (Day Trip by Car)
**Car Round Trip:** ~60 km

#### Morning: Lake Brienz Kayaking
- **Most turquoise lake in Switzerland!**
- **Launch:** Iseltwald village (iconic pier)
  - Famous Instagram location
  - Wooden pier extending into lake
- **Water:** Glacial-fed, incredible color
- **Duration:** 2-4 hours
- **Easy:** Very calm, protected

#### Lunch
- Picnic on lake shore or Iseltwald village

#### Afternoon Options
- **Giessbach Falls:**
  - 14-cascade waterfall into Lake Brienz
  - Belle Époque hotel
  - Funicular railway
  - Short hike options
  
- **Drive around lake:**
  - Scenic road both sides
  - Multiple photo stops
  - Brienz town (woodcarving center)

#### Photography/Drone
- Turquoise water with mountain reflections
- Iseltwald pier (sunrise is magical)
- Giessbach Falls
- Mountain panoramas

### Day 6: High Alps Experience (Day Trip by Car)
**Car Round Trip:** ~40 km + mountain railways

#### Option A: Jungfraujoch - Top of Europe
- **Cost:** CHF 150-200 per person (expensive!)
- **Train:** From Lauterbrunnen via Kleine Scheidegg
- **Elevation:** 3,454m
- **Duration:** Full day
- **Experience:**
  - Highest railway station in Europe
  - Ice palace
  - Sphinx Observatory viewing platform
  - Aletsch Glacier views
- **Photography:** Dramatic alpine scenery, glacier
- **Worth it:** Once-in-a-lifetime experience

#### Option B: Budget-Friendly Mountains
- **Schilthorn (Piz Gloria):**
  - James Bond filming location
  - Revolving restaurant
  - Cable car from Mürren
  - CHF 80-100 per person
  
- **Harder Kulm:**
  - Overlooks Interlaken + both lakes
  - Funicular from Interlaken
  - CHF 30-40 per person
  - Two Bridges viewpoint platform

#### Hiking Alternative
- **Free option:** Numerous valley trails
  - Männlichen to Kleine Scheidegg (panoramic)
  - Trümmelbach Falls (inside mountain)
  - Mürren village walk

## CAMPSITE HUB #3: Lake Como, Italy
**Location:** Varenna or Bellano (east shore)  
**Nights:** 2 nights (Days 7-8)  
**Move:** 250 km from Switzerland

### Day 7: Travel to Italy + Arrival
**Campervan Drive:** 250 km (3.5-4 hours via Simplon or St. Gotthard Pass)

#### Morning
- Pack up Swiss campsite
- Drive through Alps (spectacular scenery!)
- **Pass options:**
  - St. Gotthard Tunnel (easier, faster)
  - Simplon Pass (more scenic if open)

#### Afternoon
- Arrive Lake Como area
- Setup campsite
- **Varenna:** Walk the Passeggiata degli Innamorati (Lovers' Walk)
- Gelato mandatory!

#### Evening
- Lakeside dinner (Italian cuisine!)
- Explore Varenna village (colorful houses)

#### Campsite Options
- **Camping Village Conca d'Oro:** Nearby, good facilities
- **Alternative campsites:** Around Lecco area (southern end)

### Day 8: Lake Como Exploration (Full Day by Car)
**Car Round Trip:** ~80 km + ferry

#### Morning: Kayaking Lake Como
- **Launch:** Varenna area
- **Explore:**
  - Hidden coves and villas
  - Villa Monastero gardens (from water)
  - Castello di Vezio above Varenna
- **Conditions:** Generally calm (can get windy afternoon)
- **Duration:** 2-3 hours

#### Midday: Ferry to Bellagio
- **Ferry:** Varenna to Bellagio (15 minutes)
- **Bellagio:** "Pearl of Lake Como"
  - Cobblestone streets
  - Villa Melzi gardens
  - Villa Serbelloni gardens
  - Luxury shopping/cafes

#### Afternoon: Drive West Shore
- **Car ferry:** Cross to Menaggio or Cadenabbia
- **Drive:** Scenic lakeside road
- **Stops:**
  - Villa Carlotta (gardens and museum)
  - Tremezzo (beautiful views)
  - Numerous viewpoints

#### Photography/Drone
- Colorful Italian villas
- Mountains meeting water
- Bellagio from multiple angles
- Early morning reflections
- **Drone:** Check Italian regulations, avoid crowded areas

## Practical Tips

### Switzerland Essentials
- **Vignette:** Mandatory (CHF 40) - buy at border
- **Currency:** Swiss Franc (CHF) - cards widely accepted
- **Prices:** Very expensive (2-3x normal)
- **Shopping:** Do it before/after Switzerland
- **Diesel:** Most expensive in Europe

### Kayaking Specifics
- **Water temperature:** Cold! (12-18°C)
  - Wear wetsuit or layers
  - Bring dry bag for gear
- **Best conditions:** Morning (less wind)
- **Your inflatable:** Perfect for all these lakes
- **Launch:** Usually free access points

### Mountain Passes
- **Check status:** Some passes close if snow
  - Check: passsuisse.ch
- **Driving:** Low gear on descents
- **Photo stops:** Many designated viewpoints
- **Tunnels:** Alternative if passes closed

### Photography & Drone
- **Best light:** 
  - Sunrise over lakes (reflections)
  - Golden hour in mountains
- **Drone regulations:**
  - **Switzerland:** Allowed <900g in rural areas, restricted near cities
  - **Italy:** Register drone, avoid populated areas
  - **Germany:** Generally permissive in countryside
- **Weather:** Can change quickly in mountains

### Food Strategy
- **Germany:** Stock up heavily before Switzerland
- **Switzerland:** Minimize grocery shopping
  - Co-op and Migros (supermarkets)
  - Cook at campsite
- **Italy:** Enjoy dining out (reasonable prices)

## Weather Considerations

### Summer (June-August)
- **Warmest:** 20-25°C valleys, cooler mountains
- **Crowds:** Peak tourist season
- **Book ahead:** Campsites can fill up

### Shoulder Season (May, September)
- **Pros:** Fewer crowds, lower prices, beautiful
- **Cons:** Mountain passes may be closed (May)
- **September:** Often best weather!

### Rain Backup Plans
- **Museums:** Swiss Museum of Transport (Lucerne)
- **Indoor activities:** Chocolate factories, cheese dairies
- **Thermal baths:** Leukerbad (Switzerland)
- **Shopping:** Innsbruck, Lucerne
