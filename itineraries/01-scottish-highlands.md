## Practical Tips

### Driving in Scotland
- **Left side of road:** Practice at campsite if needed
- **Single-track roads:** Pull into passing places
  - Let faster traffic pass
  - Wave to oncoming drivers (Highland courtesy!)
- **Narrow:** Some roads very tight for campervan
- **Speed:** Slower than you think - enjoy the journey
- **Fuel:** Stations can be sparse - fill up opportunistically

### Weather Reality
- **Unpredictable:** Four seasons in one day!
- **Rain gear:** Essential (waterproof jacket, trousers)
- **Layers:** Fleece, windproof
- **Midges (May-September):**
  - Tiny biting insects
  - Worst in still, damp conditions
  - **Essential:** Midge net for head, Smidge repellent
  - Avon Skin So Soft (works surprisingly well!)

### Kayaking in Scotland
- **Sea kayaking:** More challenging than inland
  - Check weather, tides, conditions
  - Consider guided tours for coastal areas
- **Loch kayaking:** Generally calmer, safer for amateurs
- **Water temperature:** Cold (10-15Â°C) - dress accordingly
- **Wildlife:** Seals, otters, dolphins (if lucky!)

### Photography & Drone
- **Light:** Long twilights in summer (great for photography!)
- **Weather:** Moody conditions = dramatic photos
- **Drone regulations:**
  - Generally permissive in remote areas
  - Register with UK CAA if drone >250g
  - Avoid airports, military areas
  - Respect privacy
  - Wind can be intense - check conditions
- **Best subjects:**
  - Castles
  - Lochs and mountains
  - Coastal cliffs
  - Glencoe valley
  - Isle of Skye formations

### Scottish Culture
- **Currency:** British Pound (GBP)
  - Scottish banks issue their own notes (valid throughout UK)
- **Language:** English + Scottish Gaelic signage
- **Friendliness:** Highlanders very welcoming
- **Pubs:** Social centers, great for meals
- **Whisky:** Everywhere - try local drams!
