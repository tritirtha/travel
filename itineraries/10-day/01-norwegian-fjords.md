# Norwegian Fjords Adventure - 10 Days

**Best Season:** June-August (Midnight sun, warmest weather)  
**Total Campervan Distance:** ~3,400 km  
**Driving Style:** Long initial/return drives (600-800 km), short campsite-to-campsite moves (200-400 km)  
**Campsites:** 2-3 nights each, use car for day trips

---

## Overview

This itinerary uses a hub-and-spoke model: drive long distances to reach Norway, then stay at strategic campsites for 2-3 nights, exploring the region by car without moving the campervan daily. Perfect for relaxed exploration with your kayak, camera, and drone.

---

## Day 1: Netherlands → Northern Germany
**Drive:** 650 km (6-7 hours with breaks)  
**Overnight:** Near Hamburg/Lübeck

### Activities
- Long travel day to cover ground
- Stop in Bremen or Hamburg for lunch/fuel
- Arrive at campsite by evening
- Stock up on groceries (Germany cheaper than Norway!)

### Photography
- Historic Hanseatic architecture if time permits

### Campsite Recommendation
- Camping Schierensee (near Lübeck) - peaceful, good facilities

---

## Day 2: Germany → Denmark → Ferry
**Drive:** 500 km + ferry to Norway  
**Overnight:** Ferry or Stavanger/Haugesund, Norway

### Activities
- Drive through Denmark to Hirtshals
- **Ferry:** Hirtshals to Stavanger or Bergen (book in advance!)
  - Stavanger route: 4 hours
  - Bergen route: 16 hours (overnight ferry - great option!)
- If evening ferry, overnight on ship

### Tips
- **Critical:** Book ferry well in advance with campervan dimensions
- Stock up on food in Denmark (last chance before expensive Norway)
- If overnight ferry, you save a night's camping cost

### Ferry Options
- Fjord Line: Hirtshals-Stavanger/Bergen

## CAMPSITE HUB #1: Hardangerfjord Region
**Location:** Near Kinsarvik or Lofthus  
**Nights:** 2-3 nights (Days 3-5)  
**Campervan Stays Put**

### Day 3: Arrival & Setup
**Drive:** 200 km from Stavanger OR 100 km from Bergen  
**Setup Day**

- Drive to campsite, settle in
- Explore immediate area on foot
- Evening stroll along fjord
- Prepare for next days' adventures

### Day 4: Hardangerfjord Exploration (Day Trip by Car)
**Car Round Trip:** ~150 km

#### Activities
- **Kayaking:** Launch from multiple spots along Hardangerfjord
  - Lofthus area - calm waters, fruit orchards
  - Ulvik village - picturesque setting
  - Water crystal clear, perfect for beginners
- **Hiking:** 
  - Easy: Monk's Stairway (1 hour)
  - Moderate: Husedalen waterfalls (2-3 hours)
- **Photography:**
  - Vøringsfossen waterfall (massive cascade)
  - Fruit orchards in bloom (spring/summer)
  - Steinsdalsfossen (walk behind waterfall!)

#### Drone Opportunities
- Hardanger Bridge (longest suspension bridge in Norway)
- Fjord from above with mountain backdrop
- Orchards meeting water

### Day 5: Trolltunga Adventure (Full Day by Car)
**Car Round Trip:** ~100 km + hike

#### Option A: Trolltunga Hike (Ambitious)
- **Distance:** 28 km round trip
- **Time:** 10-12 hours
- **Difficulty:** Strenuous
- **Start:** 5:00 AM from campsite
- **Best:** June-September only
- **Photography:** Iconic cliff overhang shot
- **Drone:** Epic footage possible (check wind!)

#### Option B: Leisurely Day (If not hiking Trolltunga)
- Drive scenic FV7 road
- Visit Låtefoss (twin waterfall beside road)
- Explore Odda town
- Buarbreen glacier viewpoint
- Short hikes around area
- Return to campsite relaxed

## CAMPSITE HUB #2: Sognefjord Region
**Location:** Aurland or Flåm  
**Nights:** 2 nights (Days 6-7)  
**Move:** 200 km from Hardangerfjord campsite

### Day 6: Travel Day + Evening Exploration
**Campervan Drive:** 200 km (3-4 hours scenic)

#### Morning
- Pack up Hardangerfjord campsite
- Drive via mountain passes (stunning views)

#### Afternoon
- Arrive Aurland/Flåm area
- Setup at new campsite
- **Stegastein Viewpoint** (18 km from Aurland)
  - Architectural viewing platform
  - 600m above Aurland valley
  - Incredible photo/drone opportunity

#### Evening
- Walk around Flåm village
- Plan next day's adventures

### Day 7: Nærøyfjord & Sognefjord (Day Trip by Car)
**Car Round Trip:** ~100 km + ferry

#### Activities
- **Kayaking:** Nærøyfjord (UNESCO World Heritage Site)
  - Narrowest fjord in Europe
  - Dramatic 1,000m cliffs on both sides
  - **Rental options:** Flåm or Gudvangen
  - 3-4 hour guided or self-guided tours
  - Absolutely stunning for photography
  
- **Flåm Railway** (Optional)
  - One of world's steepest railways
  - 20 km through mountains
  - Incredible views (expensive but worth it)
  
- **Photography:**
  - Bakka church (red church on fjord)
  - Undredal (tiny village, 80 people)
  - Numerous waterfalls cascading down cliffs

#### Drone Shots
- Nærøyfjord from above (jaw-dropping)
- Village miniatures from altitude
- Ferry boats in narrow fjord

## CAMPSITE HUB #3: Geirangerfjord Area
**Location:** Geiranger village  
**Nights:** 2 nights (Days 8-9)  
**Move:** 350 km from Sognefjord (challenging but worthwhile)

### Day 8: Travel Day via Mountain Passes
**Campervan Drive:** 350 km (5-6 hours)

#### Route
- Leave Aurland early morning
- Drive via **Stryn** (lunch stop)
- Cross mountain passes (epic scenery)
- Arrive Geiranger afternoon

#### En Route Stops
- **Dalsnibba** viewpoint (if open)
  - 1,500m elevation
  - Overlooks entire Geirangerfjord
  - Toll road (worth it!)

#### Evening
- Setup campsite
- Walk to Geiranger viewpoints
- Relax after long drive

### Day 9: Geirangerfjord Exploration (Day Trip by Car)
**Car Round Trip:** ~80 km + kayaking

#### Morning: Kayaking Geirangerfjord
- **Most photographed fjord in Norway**
- **Launch:** Geiranger village
- **See:** Seven Sisters waterfall from water
- **Visit:** Abandoned farms on cliff faces (Skageflå, Knivsflå)
- **Duration:** 3-4 hours
- **Water:** Calm, turquoise, pristine
- Perfect for amateur kayakers!

#### Afternoon: Scenic Drives
- **Ørnesvingen (Eagle Road):**
  - 11 hairpin bends
  - Photography at viewpoints
  - Fjord 620m below
  
- **Trollstigen (optional extension):**
  - 90 km round trip
  - Dramatic mountain pass
  - Visitor center
  - May be closed (snow-dependent)

#### Photography/Drone
- Seven Sisters waterfall (7 separate streams)
- Geirangerfjord panorama from Ørnesvingen
- Abandoned farms clinging to cliffs
- Cruise ships in fjord (for scale)

## Campsite Recommendations

### Hardangerfjord Area (Hub #1)
- **Lofthus Camping:** Right on fjord, fruit orchards, excellent facilities
- **Kinsarvik Camping:** Central location, good for Trolltunga access

- **Flåm Camping:** Popular, great location, can be crowded
- **Aurland Fjordcamping:** Quieter, excellent views
- **Facilities:** Full facilities, near village amenities
- **Cost:** €35-45/night

### Geiranger Area (Hub #3)
- **Grande Hytteutleige & Camping:** In Geiranger village, convenient
- **Geiranger Camping:** Larger, good facilities
- **Facilities:** Showers, electricity, walking distance to center
- **Cost:** €35-45/night

## Practical Tips

### Towing & Driving in Norway
- **Mountain passes:** Use low gear on descents
- **Tunnels:** Many long tunnels (some tolled)
- **Width:** Some roads narrow - research before going
- **Speed:** Take it slow - enjoy the scenery!
- **Fuel:** Expensive in Norway (fill up in Denmark)

### Kayaking Specifics
- **Water temperature:** Cold even in summer (10-15°C)
- **Gear:** Wear layers, bring dry bag
- **Rentals:** Many places rent kayaks & gear
- **Your inflatable:** Works great in calm fjords
- **Life jackets:** Mandatory (usually provided with rentals)

### Photography & Drone
- **Light:** Midnight sun means 20+ hour daylight in summer
- **Golden hour:** Extended, amazing light
- **Drone regulations:** 
  - Generally permissive in Norway
  - Keep away from airports, military
  - Respect wildlife and privacy
  - No permit needed for <25kg recreational drones
  - Register at Luftfartstilsynet if >250g
- **Best shots:** Early morning/late evening for fewer tourists

### Weather
- **Unpredictable:** Pack layers
- **Rain gear:** Essential (it WILL rain)
- **Temperature:** 10-20°C in summer
- **Check:** yr.no (Norwegian weather service - very accurate)

### Food & Drink
- **Expensive:** Restaurants costly
- **Grocery stores:** Rema 1000, Kiwi (cheapest)
- **Alcohol:** Only sold at Vinmonopolet (state stores)
- **Water:** Tap water perfect everywhere
